THERE WAS ROT IN THE CONCRETE AND HELL HANGING IN THE AIR, AND WHENEVER I STARED UPWARDS I COULDN'T SEE THE SKY. HE WALKED DOWN FOR MILES PAST MEN IN CAGES AND THE CORPSES OF BIRDS HANGING STILL IN THE AIR, DOWN CRACKED ROADS AND ALLEYWAYS I SAW BEASTS IN, BEASTS NO ONE ELSE COULD SEE. HE WALKED AND WALKED, AS HE GOT CLOSER HE TURNED FROM BLACK TO A LIGHT RED, TO A REDDER RED, AND THEN A BRILLIANT BLUE. AND HE WALKED FINALLY, TO THE BLOCK OF ABANDONED CARPARKS LAID IN ROWS OF FIVE EACH. THERE WAS CARDBOARD COVERING THE WALLS, WALLS TALLER THAN TEN OF ME. YOU COULD KILL AND STORE THE ENTIRE CITY'S WORTH OF PEOPLE IN THE CARPARKS ALONE, STACKING THE BODIES LIKE PILES OF SAND. AND FOR TEN MINUTES ALL HE DID WAS LOOK. AND I LOOKED AT THE BACK OF HIS HEAD FOR THIS TIME. HE STOOD STILL, BOTH IN REVERENCE AND IN ITS EXACT OPPOSITE. IN A PRAYER OF HATRED.

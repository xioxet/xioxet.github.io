in the dream house
i read this at work. i like it a lot and it is good.
the fact that any person has ever written a memoir is very confusing to me. the thought of me writing something like this about my own life and publishing it and it selling millions of copies is nightmarish, i dont know how anyone is able to summon the courage to just, do it. and im grateful that there are authors who arent as cowardly as i am. the woman in the dream house is a woman whose identity can be found with a trivial google search. you ask the internet what her name is and they provide it, they have her face and her name there, you can read up on the workshops she's teaching, the programs she runs, the work she's written. its all there, and all findable, and that makes me bizarrely uncomfortable.
her description as the woman in the dream house gives some kind of vague airiness to the mental picture of her, instead of a real flesh-and-blood human she takes on this spectrelike quality, or some manifestation of domestic abuse itself, something with tendrils and smoke that snarls and breathes and fumes. when i was reading the book, thats what it felt like to me, that this profound, almost comical evil had to have come from something beyond human. when i took a break from reading it out of curiosity i googled who the woman in the dream house was and it just felt really wrong that she was a real person. i guess i also feel guilty for being compelled to look it up anyway, like i'm breaking the author's trust in me when she already trusts me so much, letting me into her life like this and i still need to take more. it felt bad.
i dont want to + am incapable of writing something important about abuse in queer relationships. the book does that, i have nothing insightful to add, nothing new that the book doesnt already say ten times better than i could. i guess the main thing i want to talk about is my own relationship with writing. oftentimes when i try to write something it will be some nonstandard bullshit, some weak attempt at an interesting concept, i grew up on the scp forums and over there this is called a 'format screw', an intentional break of an established, rigorous format (the scp document) in service of an idea the writer wants to express.
i think that these are good but they are a crutch for me and me specifically. its impossible to say in simple terms what i want to say because what i want to say is stupid, and bullshit, and trying to reach for that with my stupid bullshit writing is impossible. so it becomes necessary to couch it in high-concept high-execution ideas (kind of a misleading description, these are really easy to come up with). thinking about these ideas is really fun, and its so easy to get carried away, and oftentimes i delude myself into thinking damn, this shit straight heat, when what is really compelling is the gimmick and not the core idea underneath it.
the reason i bring this up is because this book is presented unusually (fragments of stories all labelled dream house as x or y or z) and it is a format i think is really compelling, smartly written, and strengthens the book as a whole, and i myself have probably tried writing something like this and failed miserably, and it's just, i dunno, inspiring and awing to see someone actually good at writing execute an idea very well.
i like how it is presented. it gives this dissociative segmented airiness to it, much like how the woman in the dream house doesnt feel like a real person, the fragmentary stories also just dont feel real, as if it is impossible for this to be happening to you, as if your life is not a life fully lived but a taped-together series of glass shards you stare at your own reflection in. it does feel like that this is the only possible way this book could have been written, as if continuous relentless abuse simply takes away your ability to write and express yourself, and the only way out is writing page-long summaries of what it has done to you. its good.
my hands hurt too much to continue writing the rest of this.

Maybe this will change someday. Maybe, when queerness is so normal and accepted that finding it will feel like entering paradise and more like the claiming of your own body: imperfect, but yours.

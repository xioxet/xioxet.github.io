i'm trying to figure him out. he walks a few paces in front of me, left hand stuffed into his pocket, right hand tensing and untensing to the beat of his gait. clench, unclench, clench, unclench. left step, right step. black bag hanging loose from his shoulder, bloodied feathers poking out a crack in the zipper. he walks like he's on stilts, like joints in his knee are missing, like he's fighting a beaufort-8 current that's not there. he moves with purpose. i'm trying to figure him out.

the road's long. bisects an infinite field of dead grass, cuts a path to the sun hanging corpselike in the sky, wreathed by the burial linens of clouds. we're ten miles from somewhere, five south from nowhere. when you walk out this far you reach the boundary where the city just stops. no sprawl, no dirt roads, no low-rises. a hard cut line between something and nothing. we are well into the nothing, me and him.

we're near. you see it?

i see it. oceans away from anything else, any other building, structure, five lone carparks arranged in a star over the horizon, colored the same dying gray everything else is. around four storeys tall. giant empty cinderblocks. we keep walking, walking, walking. it's the only something within the nothing for miles, the only thing to look at, fix your vision on. i walk a little faster to catch up to him, steal a glance at his eyes and they're something solemn, looking not at but through the carparks in the distance, caught in some milky haze of thought i can't decipher yet.

songs about fucking

> date: 15th apr, opinion: perfect

honestly i've just never heard these guitar tones before ever. i listen to a lot of music where they do fucked up weird shit with guitars and the stuff off of songs like the model or bad penny are just wholly, entirely, new to me, and i just think that is especially noteworthy.

a lot of my interest in this album is as a result of my interest in steve albini, who passed away at 62. i've tried listening to shellac and it just doesn't cut the same way this does. there's a.. how to say, nerdy fury to the whole thing that i can't find in the other albums. it's glasses-wearingly hateful, his voice cuts through riffs that could rend metal to shreds while he sounds a little like a pent up high schooler, and maybe this is derisive or sounds like a negative review of the music but as someone who could also be loosely described as a pent-up high schooler, this speaks truth to power, man, it does.

there's a lot i could pointlessly say about my interest in mr steve as a figure in the music industry, but the key to it is this: he was just really, really, fucking cool. this album is one of the reasons why.
illinois

> date: 24th april, rating: real good

i listened to this on a train. i had a new phone without the jack and i didnt have the necessary adapter so i had my laptop in my backpack and my headphone wires running through a slit in the zipper. it was afternoon and the sun was filtering through the windows of the train and all in all, it was a good day i enjoyed.

americans are very cloyingly romantic about america. amber waves of grain. new york concrete jungle where dreams are made of. odes to the mets. they love their cities and their homes and their states and rivers and lakes, twisters and thunderclouds and the statue of liberty and international orange and oil art therapist eggshell white office park metropolis there will always be american films and songs and books betraying an undying love for the land, big bag of stuff that gets passed through the filter paper of network television and the internet into homes in the global south, great american novels, paintings, photographs.

i feel this way about jon bois impassionedly talking about the mariners. i don't give a fucking shit about the seattle mariners but i know who ichiro suzuki is now. i feel this way about the virgin suicides, how those suburban homes may as well be on mercury with how different they are from what's here. its interesting. i hate my country of residence. i deny myself the demonym, i hate its buildings and skyscrapers and systems and ways of life, frankly i hate its people and dislike them by default. i hate it so much i describe it as my country of residence instead of my country full stop. i could never imagine feeling this way about a place. places are places. places are settings, inert, present and permanently-background.

places are romantic, far-away places in movies, but not the one i live in right now. this place is a place, other places are places. so when sufjan stevens sings about getting his heart broken in chicago, the swirling rivers out in the wilderness, corpses buried under a house, the way he animates 72 minutes of songwriting in a way that makes the state of illinois breathe, it's something to think about. maybe something to aspire to.

enough about places. my favorite song here is palisades. most of the music i listen to, the lyrics dont play a particularly important role, a side effect of the genres i like the most. palisades is as beautiful as the rest of this album instrumentally but the reason its my favorite is because - well - sometimes good writing is revelatory. i value that a lot in writing, the threshold separating 'something that is great' and 'something i carry with me forever' is if it reveals something to me personally. if there's some choice of words, some simple combination i could not ever find myself but has been placed before me, written by someone else, and it speaks to some truth i have been trying to gnaw at or grasp at for years of my life, then i will remember that.

palisades is about a blossoming gay relationship and all those complications that come with it. it's about being young and haunted, and as much as i try to write about the exact same thing i just never will cut to the core of it the way mr sufjan does. i will simply never write those lines.

five stars for palisades alone.
